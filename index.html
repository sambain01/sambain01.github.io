Hybrid Spray Drone

The Demand

Invasive weed species such as wilding pines are continuing to take over large areas of farming and conservation land within New Zealand. The current methods of eradicating these species – by way of helicopter spraying and ground control – are expensive and time consuming.

Challenge

To design a remotely operated aerial system capable of spraying targeted weeds over expansive areas.

Solution

A hybrid petrol – electric spray drone, capable of carrying ten liters of spray alongside five liters of petrol, achieving flight times of up to fifty minutes. The drone features removeable arms for easy transportation, 360 degrees LIDAR based collision avoidance system, and full engine and system monitoring via the controller.

My Contribution

My main contribution was as the mechanical design lead for this project. This included designing of rapid mount and disconnect features, vibration isolating components, design for blow molding, design for rapid prototyping using in house additive manufacturing, laser cutting and machining processes, finite element analysis. I also aided in developing bespoke obstacle avoidance algorithms, as well as custom load cell modules for accurately measuring and reporting the fluid level in each of the three tanks.

  • C++ STM32 Embedded Development
  • Rapid Design and Prototyping

Smart Battery Charging Case

The Demand

Aeronavics required the integration of a new battery management system, in order to provide battery protection mechanisms, and advanced health monitoring, for ultimate safety of our electric and hybrid RPAS platforms.

Challenge

To design and integrate a battery management system on a custom printed circuit board and integrate it mechanically to a lithium-ion battery pack. Furthermore, a custom charging/transportation case was required for safe storage and charging of the batteries.

Solution

A fully integrated battery and charging case was developed. The battery included automated health and state of charge monitoring, which was communicated to aircraft or charging case via CANBUS. Furthermore, the batteries incorporated comprehensive failsafe algorithms in order to avoid damage and maintain user safety during over-current, over-temperature or extreme voltage conditions.

The battery charging case features intelligent charging modes to minimise charge time without compromising battery health or causing temperature concerns. The charging case also allows active discharge modes, maintaining the batteries at an optimal charge level for travel and long term storage.

My Contribution

My contribution to this project was extensive, including the full electrical, mechanical and software design of the battery management system. I also led the development of the mechanical and software systems for the battery charging case. A major aspect of this section of the project was the development of thermal regulation hardware in order to facilitate safe and rapid charging and active discharging functionality.

Radar Obstacle Avoidance

The Demand

Vision and LIDAR based avoidance systems are unable to provide robust obstacle tracking in unideal environments such as poor weather and extreme lighting conditions. Radar poses a potential detection system that is complementary to the LIDAR detection systems currently used on Aeronavics aircrafts.

Challenge

To investigate the effectiveness of radar detection systems currently on the market and integrate a detection system into Aeronavics aircraft.

Solution

A custom radar module PCB was developed, based on a Texas Instruments 77GHz industrial radar sensor IC, containing a DSP, MCU and hardware accelerator. The modules can detect and locate small obstacles such as low voltage power lines at distances of up to 30 meters with a reliability of 95% within this range, while also detecting other aircraft at a range of up to 200 meters. Each module can simultaneously track the position of up to 30 obstacles in 3d space and report this data back to the primary flight controller over CANBUS using CAN-FD protocols.

My Contribution

Myself and a fellow coworker worked together to design the printed circuit board to be used for the project. After reviewing and testing the board in isolation, it was my job to adapt the open source software on the integrated imu/dsp chip, in order to output the 3D obstacle messsages to the drones CANBUS.

Upon validation of the new software, I then began full integration flight testing, in order to quantify the detection rate of the new sensors. Once I had formed an accurate model of the sensors capabilities, I adapted the drones avoidance algorithms in order to optimize them for safety and reliability.

Key Skills

  • High Frequency PCB Design
  • CAN-FD protocol development
  • Testing and Reporting

Wifi Weather Station

The Demand

My parents live on a farm in a remote area, and like to keep track of rainfall and temperature, in order to optimise their pasture management and fertiliser application.

Traditionally they were using a manual rain gauge and logging the data on a handwritten spreadsheet. This required daily attention in order to get accurate data, and extracting anything meaningful from the data was difficult and time consuming.

Challenge

To design a wireless weather station, capable of measuring and reporting rainfall and temperature every hour of the day. The weather station should require no external power or battery changes, and should be able to report the data to a database stored in the cloud for displaying on a custom webapp.

Solution

A custom weather station was developed utilising a tip bucket rain gauge design, paired with an esp32 module. The design utilises the onboard hall effect sensor for bucket tip detection, along with the onboard wifi module for reporting the data to a firebase database.

The design utilises an extremely small 150mW solar panel, which integrates cleanly with the design. In order to sustain its battery level with such low input power, the module utilises esp32's low power subprocessor for registering bucket tips, while waking up once per hour to upload to the database. During periods of extended lack of sunlight when the battery level becomes depleted, the module stores the data in flash until the battery level restabilises, and the data is bulk uploaded to the database. In this mode, the weather station can last 200 days off a single 18650 battery.
